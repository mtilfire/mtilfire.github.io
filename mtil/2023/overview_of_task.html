MTIL (2023)

Machine Translation for Indian Languages


The participants of this program will have access to a comprehensive set of resources including development, training, and test datasets. There are two main tasks in this program.

Task A focuses on machine translation in a general domain. Participants are required to download the provided training data and develop a machine translation system capable of translating from language A to language B. The participants are expected to submit their translation results to the leaderboard for evaluation.

Task B offers more flexibility, allowing participants to utilize either a limited amount of parallel data or monolingual data in a specific domain. Alternatively, they can employ other feasible techniques to build machine translation systems for various domains, such as healthcare and governance. These domains pose additional challenges in modeling, making this task particularly intriguing.

By participating in these tasks, participants will have the opportunity to showcase their expertise in machine translation, contribute to the advancement of translation technology, and tackle the difficulties associated with domain-specific translation problems.

Introduction


Forum for Information Retrieval and Evaluation (FIRE) was started in 2008 with the aim of building a common platform for promoting research and evaluation in the field of Information Retrieval and Natural Language Processing in South Asia. FIRE aims to build a South Asian counterpart of TREC, CLEF and NTCIR. FIRE has evolved continuously to meet the new challenges in multilingual information access. It has expanded to include new domains like legal information access, social media analytics, financial text analytics, plagiarism detection, mixed script information retrieval and spoken document retrieval to name a few.

Every year FIRE offers peer reviewed conference track, evaluation tracks and tutorials. The peer reviewed conference track consists of papers from searching and ranking, recommendation systems, document representation, evaluation and explainability. The evaluation tracks consist of shared tasks which deal with different research areas like hate speech detection, summarization, code to comment generation, fake news detection, anaphora resolution. FIRE also offers tutorials which cover the latest topics in information retrieval and NLP like explainability, knowledge graphs and chatbots. FIRE also has invited keynote speakers’ sessions where renowned researchers in IR and NLP give a talk on their recent research works.
